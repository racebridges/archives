EXPLORING THE STORY


Summary

Thirty teenagers from twenty countries, one Jewish teacher, and one Cuban-Irish-American storyteller (story artist, Antonio Sacre) set out to publish a book of writing in one of the poorest and most challenging high schools in Los Angeles. Will fear and distrust stop the project before it begins, or will they stand together?

Some of the most poignant and beautiful writings are created by students simply sharing their life circumstances with one another. Powerful and moving, this story told by Antonio Sacre is a true personal experience that shows that anything is possible and that all students should dream big. Listen as Antonio relates his time spent with a class of high school seniors, the connection he made with them, and their remarkable achievements.

Comments & Reflections

“However, consciously or unconsciously, tensions and conflicts inevitably arise among confreres due to generational gaps, racism, ethnocentrism, personality differences and stereotyping. Confreres may be tempted to avoid the challenge of enriching intercultural experiences… either by imposing a uniform lifestyle, or by living in mutual indifference…”
Documents of the 17th General Chapter SVD 2012

“30 kids from 20 countries…
Speak secrets.
Reveal wonder !
You do not stand alone.
We stand together…”

(Antonio Sacre in video story)

  • This story describes a resistant classroom of high school students from many countries who sit in frozen opposition to Antonio Sacre’s attempts to motivate and inspire them. Facing diversity to create a unified project is the challenge. Can you name projects that you have struggled with and then succeeded like this ? What was the “bridge” that lead to cooperation and success ?
  • In Antonio’s story essays are used to surface very diverse stories into an anthology of variety and hope. The written and expressive arts do provide tools that can connect wide differences and histories. This story of Antonio and his class is an example. Do you have examples ? A play ? Poetry ? A work of art that combines talents such as a class or community mural ?
  • An anthology of stories in your house, parish or community could be a tool that can lead to connection and celebration.
