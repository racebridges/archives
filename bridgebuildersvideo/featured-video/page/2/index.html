SHADOWBALL

Posted on 09/20/2018 by bridgebuildersvideo

by Storyteller Bobby Norfolk

Bobby Norfolk’s living history performance called “Shadowball” takes us on a journey through the heyday of the Negro Baseball Leagues. This was a time when the USA was segregated by Jim Crow laws, and America’s “National Pastime” wore two faces, the whites-only Major Leagues and the upstart Negro National League.  This video is an excerpt from his longer story performance.

Bobby brings to life an important but sometimes forgotten era in baseball history, telling passionate, humorous, and often unbelievable stories of exploits both on and off the field, focusing on the St. Louis Stars own James “Cool Papa” Bell, and the Kansas City Monarch’s Leroy “Satchel” Paige. Bobby invites us to ponder the harsh discrimination, adversity, and eventual Hall of Fame triumph for some of these players.

Learn what the term “Shadowball” meant if you were a person of color who played baseball in segregated America in the 1920’s and 30’s.  Length ~6 minutes.
