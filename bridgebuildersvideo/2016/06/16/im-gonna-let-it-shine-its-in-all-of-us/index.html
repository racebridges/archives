I’M GONNA LET IT SHINE: IT’S IN ALL OF US

Posted on 06/16/2016 by bridgebuildersvideo

by Storyteller Bill Harley 
Bill gathers a group of musicians and singers together to record an album of Civil Rights Freedom Songs.  However, they learn that they can’t assume they are all on the same page or that underlying emotions and biases aren’t in play. Length: 12 mins.

EXPLORING THE STORY

Summary
Master storyteller and talented musician Bill Harley recalls the time when he was a community organizer. He remembers a daunting project where he gathered talented singers and musicians, unknown and well-known, for a professional recording of Freedom Songs from the Civil Rights Movement. The gathering of those multi-racial singers to make that recording provided a moment when everyone present had to acknowledge their biases and racism.

Comments & Reflections

“However, consciously or unconsciously, tensions and conflicts inevitably arise among confreres due to generational gaps, racism, ethnocentrism, personality differences and stereotyping. Confreres may be tempted to avoid the challenge of enriching intercultural experiences… either by imposing a uniform lifestyle, or by living in mutual indifference…”
Documents of the 17th General Chapter SVD 2012

“(Going around the circle of performers for comments)…. People were very
polite when we were going around the circle…the things they were saying was to be
safe…. But that’s no way to sing Freedom Songs. And trying to make sure you don’t
make any mistakes is not the way to do what’s right. I could feel the tension in the room
rising and thinking this is beyond myself…. I can’t fix this …..”

(From the video-story by Bill Harley)

 

Ponderings

Bill Harley’s story recalls the words of one of the participants in the stumbling, multi-racial creative process that led to the recording of the Freedom Songs.  The statement that participant Hollis Watkins made was:

“…My fear is that we are not going to admit that we are racist. ……. I want us all to admit that we are all racist.  How could we not be?  Look where we have been raised.  Everybody in this room doesn’t  want to be.  We all are here because we don’t want to be.  It’s not what we want to be….. But when we admit it we can get past it…And you could feel everyone in that room ….  Breathe.”

This story is about a discovery process happening with 30 creative people gathered to make a recording of Freedom Songs.  Emerging from this session is the fact that we all have our biases (racist and otherwise).   How we manage them is the litmus test.

How and when do we confess/share our biases/racist/homophobic reactions so that we can manage them and seek to leave them behind ?
