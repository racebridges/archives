BECOMING A WOMAN OF COLOR: DISCOVERY IN THE PHILIPPINES

Posted on 04/17/2018 by bridgebuildersvideo

by Storyteller Rebecca Mabanglo-Mayor
Rebecca, a Filipino American, grew up in nearly all-white neighborhoods and schools in the Seattle area. In 2000, she began reconnecting with her Filipino heritage and became —  a woman of color. This is a story of discovery. Of deeper and enriching roots.   Length ~5 minutes.

“However, consciously or unconsciously, tensions and conflicts inevitably arise among confreres due to generational gaps, racism, ethnocentrism, personality differences and stereotyping. Confreres may be tempted to avoid the challenge of enriching intercultural experiences… either by imposing a uniform lifestyle, or by living in mutual indifference…”
 Documents of the 17th General Chapter SVD 2012

“ … I didn’t see myself as a woman of color but I saw myself  more as a white man, because that was the only way that I could succeed…..

…. I have been shaped by many different forces. The dreams of my parents. Society’s expectations of what a woman, an Asian woman, a Filipina, should be.  But all those are external. All those things are not me. The stories of my ancestors helped me understand that I am first myself. I am Filipino and I am American.  I am who I am. And as I express who I am. And I do..as I am… that is all that is needed.”

(From the video story by Rebecca Mabanglo-Mayor)

Pondering the Story

Discussion Questions:

  • What is the story of your family’s immigration to North America and settlement? If your heritage is Native American, where did your tribe(s) live before colonization?
  • Where in the world has your family lived? How are race and culture connected to those places?
  • What are the stereotypes about your race, gender, economic status, or geographic location? Do you feel these stereotypes reflect who you are or create pressures to be something you are not?
  • How often do you see yourself or your heritage portrayed in films? What do the main characters in these films look like and what do they have to do to succeed?
