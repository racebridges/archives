SPARTA, GEORGIA

Posted on 09/15/2015 by bridgebuildersvideo

by Storyteller Gene Tagaban
A young Indian, Native American man travels by van across the southern USA to see the land of his people.  Along his journey, he has the experience of meeting a southern white couple on a back country dirt road; he plays basketball with local children; he connects with an old black man in Sparta, Georgia who fought with First Nation men during the Korean War.  In his journey he finds – brotherhood.  Length – 9 minutes

Told by Gene Tagaban

EXPLORING THE STORY

Summary

Storyteller Gene Tagaban is from Alaska.  His rich family heritage is in the Native American Tinglit and Cherokee peoples as well as being part Filipino.   In his high energy style of storytelling Gene takes us on a journey he made through America’s deep south.  A Native American meets White and Black southerners.  Basketball with some kids unites.  Finally a warm meeting with Uncle LeRoy, a vet from the Korean war results in the richest gift of all — brotherhood.

Comments & Reflections

“However, consciously or unconsciously, tensions and conflicts inevitably arise among confreres due to generational gaps, racism, ethnocentrism, personality differences and stereotyping. Confreres may be tempted to avoid the challenge of enriching intercultural experiences… either by imposing a uniform lifestyle, or by living in mutual indifference…”
Documents of the 17th General Chapter SVD 2012

“Are you an Indian? …”
“Can we touch you?”
“You want to touch me ?”
“…So they felt my skin. They felt my hair…
They ran off and brought their families, their relatives, aunts, cousins.
They wanted to meet us…Native American people…
Because they had only heard about us in movies, books, magazines, museums.
They never met a real live Native person before…”

From the video-story by Gene Tagaban

 

This story is about unexpected surprises and delights emerging from interracial discovery.
Worth pondering after hearing this story :

  • What are the unexpected surprises and delights have you found in places of racial or cultural difference?
  • Have you ever made a journey outside of your usual routes and destinations into and through a “country” of “different” people ?  Not because of a document or an expectation of others, but because within you is the urge to search for a richer and wider version of God’s family and world ?
  • What are the fears ?  What are the inner gifts received ?
